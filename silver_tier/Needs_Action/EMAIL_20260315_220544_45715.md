Applying jitter to the uncapped exponential delay and then clamping (`min(..., max_delay)`) makes retries deterministic once `initial_delay * multiplier**(attempt-1)` grows well past `max_delay` (for defaults, this starts at attempt 5), because the jittered value is always above the cap and every call returns exactly `max_delay`. This removes the backoff randomness that spreads client retries and can increase synchronized retry bursts under load; clamping the base delay before jitter (and still capping the final value) avoids exceeding `max_delay` while keeping jitter effective.
